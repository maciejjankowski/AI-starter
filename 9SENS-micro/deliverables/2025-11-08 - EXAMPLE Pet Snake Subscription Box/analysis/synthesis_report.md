## Executive Summary

The pet snake subscription box is **marginally viable as standalone business** but **highly viable as beachhead for exotic pet care platform**.

Market analysis reveals a niche opportunity (500K US snake owners, 8% CAGR) with limited competition (2 players, both struggling). However, unit economics are weak (LTV/CAC = 2.95x) and churn risk is extreme (65% Year 1 per competitor data).

**BILL** validated market size but flagged high churn as pattern across niche pet subscriptions. **NASH** calculated expected values showing platform pivot delivers 10x higher EV ($1.5M vs $150K for subscription-only). **STEVE** recommended strategic pivot: use subscription as customer acquisition for higher-margin exotic pet care platform.

**Bottom line**: Don't build pure subscription. Build platform with subscription as entry product.

## Key Insights

### From BILL (Data Validation)

**Market Reality**:
- Validated market: 500K US snake owners (Pet Industry Joint Advisory Council 2024)
- Growth: 8% CAGR (exotic pets outpacing traditional pets)
- Addressable market: 75K (500K × 15% interested in subscription)

**Pattern Detected**:
- Niche pet subscription boxes have 65% Year 1 churn
- Root cause: Novelty wears off, products become repetitive
- 2 competitors: ReptileBox (shut down 2023), SnakeCrate (<2K subs after 3 years)

**Assumption Exposed**:
- Claim: "Snake owners are underserved"

**Data Gap**:

- $49-79 estimate based on dog box pricing (unvalidated)

**Opportunity Identified**:

## Critical Assumptions to Validate

### 1. Snake owners will buy consumables online
**Impact**: HIGH
**Confidence**: 0.60 (medium)
**Test Method**:
- Survey 50 snake owners
- Ask: "Where do you currently buy frozen mice, supplements, equipment?"
- Ask: "Would you buy online if 20% cheaper + delivered?"
- **Success criteria**: >60% say yes

### 2. Community engagement drives retention
**Impact**: HIGH
**Confidence**: 0.50 (low)
**Test Method**:
- Build simple forum/Facebook group for first 100 subscribers
- Track: Active users, posts per week, repeat purchases
- Compare: Retention of engaged users vs non-engaged
- **Success criteria**: Engaged users retain >50% vs 35% baseline

### 3. Marketplace commission model generates sufficient margin
**Impact**: MEDIUM
**Confidence**: 0.70 (medium-high)
**Test Method**:
- Research: What commission % do exotic pet marketplaces charge?
- Survey: Would breeders list on marketplace at 15% commission?
- Benchmark: Etsy (5%), eBay (10-15%), niche marketplaces (15-25%)
- **Success criteria**: Breeders willing at 15%, generates >30% margin

## GO/NO-GO Decision

### Recommendation: **PIVOT**

**DO NOT** build pure subscription business.

**DO** build exotic pet care platform with these steps:

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3)**: MVP Subscription Box
- Launch simple subscription box ($39/mo)
- Target: 100 subscribers (validate demand + gather data)
- Metrics: Track retention, identify churn reasons, survey consumables interest

**Phase 2 (Months 3-6)**: Add Consumables
- Launch Shopify store for frozen mice, supplements, equipment
- Offer 20% discount to subscribers
- Metrics: Upsell conversion rate (target >20%), AOV, repeat purchase rate

**Phase 3 (Months 6-12)**: Build Marketplace
- Launch breeder/seller marketplace
- Charge 15% commission on transactions
- Build community features (forums, care guides, vet network)
- Metrics: Seller signups, transaction volume, marketplace GMV

**Phase 4 (Year 2+)**: Platform Expansion
- Expand to all exotic reptiles (lizards, turtles, etc.)
- Add premium subscription tiers with exclusive marketplace access
- Metrics: Multi-product customers, LTV expansion, margin improvement

## IF No-Go → Why Kill This Idea

(This section only applies if validation fails)

**Kill signals**:
- Survey shows <40% interest in online consumables
- Upsell conversion <10%
- Retention stays <40% even with community
- Marketplace commission model rejected by breeders

**Why it failed**:
- Market too small for subscription-only model
- Churn too high to achieve profitability
- No defensible moat without platform
- Unable to expand beyond initial niche

**Alternative pivots to consider**:
- B2B: Sell software to exotic pet stores
- Content: Build media brand, monetize via ads/sponsorships
- Different niche: Apply platform model to different exotic pets
